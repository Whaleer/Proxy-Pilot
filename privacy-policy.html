Privacy Policy for ProxyPilot

Effective Date: April 10, 2025

Introduction

ProxyPilot is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle information when you use our Chrome extension.

Information Collection

We do not collect any personal information. ProxyPilot does not collect, store, or transmit any browsing data, personal information, or usage statistics. All proxy configurations and domain rules are stored locally on your device using Chrome's storage API.

Required Permissions

ProxyPilot requires certain permissions to function properly:

  • Proxy Permission: Required to modify and control browser proxy settings, allowing the extension to switch connection methods based on user-configured rules.
  • Tabs Permission: Enables the extension to identify the URL and domain information of the currently active tab to apply the correct proxy rules.
  • Storage Permission: Necessary to save user preferences, proxy configurations, and domain-specific rules locally.
  • ActiveTab Permission: Allows temporary access to the current tab's information only when you interact with the extension.
  • Host Permission: Enables monitoring and intercepting network requests to apply the correct proxy settings for specific domains.

Data Storage

All data is stored locally on your device using Chrome's storage API. We do not use external servers to store your data.

Third-Party Services

ProxyPilot does not integrate with any third-party services or analytics platforms.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the effective date.

Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact us via GitHub at https://github.com/Whaleer/Proxy-Pliot.
